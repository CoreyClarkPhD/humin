Educational Sequences

Peru Levels Overview

Level

Type

Letter Sound

1R-controlled Pattern vowels (Vr)AR, ARSE, ER, ERSE, IR, OR, ORE, ORSE, UR, URSE
2Silent-E Pattern vowels (VCe)ACe, OCe, ICe, UCe, ECe
3Variants-ck, -Vke, -ce, -tch, -ge, -dge
4Long A wordsAI, AY, (ACe, AR)
5Long O wordsOA, OW, (OCe, OR)
6Long E wordsEA, EE, (ECe, ER)

Vowel Pattern Crystals

Vowel Pattern
Letter Combinations
CVC crystal (short vowel)

a, e, i , o , u
- short vowel sound
- many combinations of consonants before and after the vowel

Vtch - atch, etch, itch, otch, utch
- is in level 3 to show how ch/tch are a signal to CVC pattern.

Vdge - adge, edge, idge, odge, udge
- is in level 3 to show how dge/j sounds are a signal to CVC pattern.

Vnge - ange, enge, inge, onge, unge
- is in level 3 to show how n+j/g sounds are a signal to CVC pattern.

Vr Crsytal (r-controlled)

ar, er, ir, or, ur
- r-controlled, long vowel sound

Vrse - arse, erse, irse, orse, urse 
- r-controlled, long vowel sound
- The e at the end has to do with grammar not pronunciation of the vowel,  If there is a consonant+vowel+r+s you need the +e to make it singular instead of plural.

Vrge - arge, erge, irge, orge, urge

VCe Crystal (silent-e)

aCe, eCe, iCe, oCe, uCe
- silent e, long vowel sound

Vke - ake, eke, ike, oke, uke
- silent e, long vowel sound
- level 3 has an example of this to compare Vck (short vowel, CVC) to Vke (long vowel, VCe) that both make a hard K sound. 

Vce - ace, ece, ice, oce, uce
- silent e, long vowel sound
- in level 3 to show that Vce makes the letter c pronounced like the letter s when there is an e at the end.

Vge - age, ege, ige, oge, uge
- silent e, long vowel sound
- in level 3 to show that g sounds can have different vowel sounds depending on what is around them.
(- Vdge, Vnge -short vowel, like CVC
- Vge - long vowel, regular VCe
- Vrge - r-controlled, long vowel sound, like Vr)

A Crystal (long a)

ai, ay, a (if it is alone in the first syllable of a two syllable word.  Example:  acorn (a-corn) or paper (pa-per).)
- long a sound

O Crystal (long o)oa, ow, oe, o (if it is alone at the first syllable of a two syllable word.  Example:  over (o-ver) or robot (ro-bot).)
- long o sound

E Crystal (long e)

ee, ea, e (if at the end of a one syllable word.  Example:  be, he, me.  If the first syllable of a two syllable word.  Example:  even (e-ven) or delete (de-lete).)
